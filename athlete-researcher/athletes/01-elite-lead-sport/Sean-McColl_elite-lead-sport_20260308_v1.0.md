### 原文全文

**Weekly training volume:**
10–15 hours total. "I don't say I have to train 10 hours a week. I play it extremely by ear."

**Climbing sessions:**
4–5 days/week × 1.5–2.5 hours. Focus on boulder problems at all difficulty levels.

**Conditioning (2–3×/week):**
- Front levers and L-sits
- Knee lifts and planks
- Ab-focused circuits

**Finger Training (up to 3×/week):**
Dead hangs, alternating-arm protocol, progressing to two-handed hangs.

**Cross-Training:**
- Theraband exercises (20 min to 1 hour) daily for lower body
- Cycling twice weekly (~40 min each direction)

**Circuit Training Method for Lead Season:**
"I'll do 25 to 60 moves and try them 'x' amount of times." For 30-move circuits: 4 reps, 6-min rest, repeat, 20-min breaks between sets.

**Annual structure:**
- Jan–Mar: Intensive training
- Apr–Nov: World Cup season (bouldering → lead discipline transitions)
- December: Complete rest (1 month off, "just forget about training and go climbing with friends outdoors, with no pressure")

**Nutrition:**
"I eat healthy. I eat all foods." Multiple daily meals emphasizing protein, carbs, vegetables. 9 hours nightly sleep. Avoids sugar and alcohol during training.
