### 原文全文

**On relief after Tokyo Olympics (no more speed):**
"An incredible lightness since the Olympic Games in Tokyo were over and I no longer have to climb speed."

**On training and current condition:**
"Extremely fit and perhaps better prepared than ever before." Sustained injury-free preparation for 2022 season.

**On identity as competitor:**
"Until now I've always been more of a competitive climber than a rock climber, even if there's a bit of a shift happening now... I genuinely loves competition and the preparation process, setting ambitious goals and training to improve continuously."

**On outdoor vs competition crossover:**
"When I'm in competition mode, fully preparing for a competition, it brings enormous fitness. That helps on the rock." Conversely, outdoor climbing teaches efficiency lessons unavailable on artificial walls.

**On wanting to climb 9c:**
"If you want to be one of the best climbers in the world, you have to prove yourself on the rock." Specific ambition: achieve 9c grade, joining the exclusive group.

**On Paris 2024 qualification being harder:**
"Even more difficult than 2019 in Japan." New qualifying series structure with later timing in 2024.

**On European Championships Munich (new Olympic format):**
"Having the European Championships on the doorstep is good, but because of the premiere of the new Olympic format, it's all the more of a highlight for me."

### 摘要 / 备注

> 东京奥运后至2022赛季开始的深度访谈。核心点：脱离速度攀的解脱感；以比赛为中心的身份认同；竞技训练与户外攀登的互补关系；对9c的明确野心。

**Season planning:**
Primary focus: multiple Bouldering World Cups before Tokyo, skipping American events. Lead training: Innsbruck, Villars, Chamonix. Speed: "I keep doing two short sessions a week all the time."

**Speed goal:**
Personal best 6.82 seconds. Target before Olympics: 6.5 seconds. Focus: technique refinement in specific sequences.

**Medal aspirations:**
Primary goal: securing a medal, ideally gold. Competition analysis: identified Tomoa as overwhelming favorite, Adam Ondra as significant contender. "The unpredictable combined format" with specific wall design ("only one overhang, one slab and a big vertical part") meant many athletes could potentially medal.

**Heat preparation:**
Planned targeted acclimation training and cooling strategies. Personal preference: warmer climbing environments.
