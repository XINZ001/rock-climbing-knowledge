### 原文全文

**Kneebar Development**
Ondra credited specific calf training as transformative. He performed interval exercises with toes elevated, knees bent at 90 degrees, holding 40-second intervals. This allowed him to hang in kneebars for extended periods, enabling crucial rest opportunities during the climb.

**Physical Therapy Collaboration**
Working with physiotherapist Klaus, Ondra addressed three specific movement challenges:
- Calf strengthening for kneebar hanging
- Side abdominal work for precise foot placement
- Shoulder stabilization exercises

**Route-Specific Training**
Ondra trained exclusively for Silence from May through September across seven trips totaling approximately 60–70 days on the route.

**Climbing Style Insights**
Ondra emphasizes fast, efficient climbing rather than brute force: "I developed this climbing style which is risky" but maintains relative relaxation throughout sequences, avoiding unnecessary power waste.

**The Route's Characteristics**
Silence features multiple distinct sections:
- First 20 meters graded 8b with a significant kneebar
- Crux 1: Ten-move boulder problem (V15 equivalent)
- Crux 2: V13 boulder section with challenging undercling
- Crux 3: V10 boulder problem

**Mental Approach**
Ondra utilizes visualization extensively before attempts. He practices mental rehearsal of successful ascents and enters an "autopilot" state, allowing intuition and experience to guide decision-making rather than conscious thought.

**Weekly Training Structure**
- Monday–Thursday: Hard training days
- Friday: Rest day
- Saturday–Sunday: Outdoor climbing

Daily components include campus boarding (40 min), bouldering circuits, endurance intervals, and supplementary fitness with rings and TRX.

**Diet**
Modified nutrition to increase protein intake, eating fish daily. Limits food quantity despite substantial appetite to maintain optimal climbing weight.

### 摘要 / 备注

> 围绕 Silence 首登的专项训练深度采访。关键点：1）为一条路线专门练小腿肌肉和 kneebar；2）与物理治疗师合作针对路线动作；3）60-70天的专项练习；4）心理"自动驾驶"状态。

### 原文全文（节选核心段落，完整文字稿约8000字）

**On starting climbing:**
"Since I was six I've been climbing four or five times a week... Since I was seven, pretty much, I knew I wanted to be a professional rock climber."

**On early training philosophy (no campus board until 17):**
"Until I was 17, just climbing. It meant a lot of bouldering, a lot of longer problems on a bouldering wall like 15 or 20 moves, and a lot of circuits... I hadn't done any campus board until I was 16 or 17. The only way I trained until I was 16 or 17 was just by climbing."

"Even when I was 10 I was thinking in a smart way. I thought that if I campused at that moment it would make me climb harder in that moment but at the same time, it would cause me injury that could be with my body for the rest of my life."

**On starting training with Patxi Usobiaga:**
"There are not so many coaches that I would trust and the only coach that I could trust was Patxi Usobiaga, my friend and very, very good ex-competitor... We started working last spring and – that was something totally different."

"I have never trained as hard as I did last year... I knew that I had a next competition in two months and I could take advantage of these two months for really hard training."

**On periodized training structure (4-week blocks):**
"Four-week blocks, pretty much. Four-week blocks of endurance, then four-weeks block of power, then four-weeks block of a period when I wasn't training that hard and just waiting for the shape to come."

**On training frequency — 3-4 sessions per day:**
"I would try to wake up as early as possible, go for the morning session, then go back to school, then go back for training, then go back for lunch, then go back to school, then go training, take some rest, and training again."

Sessions structure: each session 45 min–1 hour, one focus per session (power = bouldering + campusing; power endurance = bouldering/campus; endurance = wall circuits). Minimum 1 hour rest between sessions.

**On campus board frequency (5+ times/week):**
"At least five times. Some days I had one session campus and some days I had even two sessions of campus."
"It's very dangerous, for sure, but if I want to search my limits I always need to be on this border when the injuries are close but at the same time I'm careful."

**On why he avoids fingerboard:**
"I've never found fingerboarding very efficient. Maybe it's because I have been training for a long time just by climbing itself outdoors, a lot of times on limestone which was pretty fingery so my fingers are, I think, my strength."

**On TRX / no weightlifting:**
"The only gym kind of exercises that I do are on the TRX. I think working with your own weight is more natural."
Uses 4kg weight vest for certain training periods, but only when not close to competition.

**On diet:**
"Lunch I eat some carbohydrates with vegetables. Dinner, some protein with vegetables." Eats meat max twice/week. Uses superfoods (goji, raw cocoa, hemp seed, chia). Current weight: 68kg, varies 66–70.

**On school + climbing combination:**
"I was climbing, then I go to school and if I go to school I know that I'm totally wrecked from climbing but at the same time I just occupy my mind with something else. Otherwise, if I just went home and I would just think about how tired I am, that wouldn't make me feel any better."

**On the mental side:**
"The main motivation is not for sure to beat the others but to beat my own previous performance... I would say of course maybe 60% of why I love climbing is the challenge part of it."

**On 9c being possible (said in 2015!):**
"I think 9c is possible. Maybe not right now... I knew from summer 2013 when I was in Flatanger where I was working on several projects which could be 9c, I did all the moves in these projects but I just felt it wasn't possible at that moment."

### 摘要 / 备注

> 迄今最详细的 Adam Ondra 训练方法第一手档案（2015年）。关键数据：6岁开始、17岁才上 campus board、与 Patxi 合作后开始结构化训练、每天3-4次训练（各45-60分钟）、不做指力板、使用4公斤重训背心、每周6天训练。

### 原文全文

Three visualization approaches Ondra describes:
1. Stay still and only do everything in your head
2. Move hands and feet slightly while you picture the moves
3. Ask someone to "create" crucial holds and footholds — get your body into position and have a friend create the jug exactly where it would be on the wall

"The more you visualize, the more you have it dialed and the easier the route feels, and you can try it 20 times in real life then 200 times in your mind with the same result as if you'd tried the climb 50 times in real life."

Training frequency: 15 minutes daily to visualization + 40 minutes to supplementary exercises designed for the hardest route sections. Work with physiotherapist Klaus Isele emphasized building automatic muscle memory for correct movement patterns.

### 摘要 / 备注

> 三种可视化训练法详解，以及为 Silence 每天做15分钟心理预演+40分钟体能补充训练的具体方法。
