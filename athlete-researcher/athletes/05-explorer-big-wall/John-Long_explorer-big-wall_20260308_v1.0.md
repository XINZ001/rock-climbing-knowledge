### 原文全文（英文连贯版）

Born July 21, 1953, in Indio, California, John Long graduated from Upland High School in 1971 and studied humanities at the University of LaVerne, Claremont Graduate School, and Claremont School of Theology.

**Climbing Achievements**

*The Nose One-Day Ascent (1975)*

Long achieved "the first one-day ascent of the most sought-after rock climb in North America, the 3,000-foot Nose route on El Capitan" on Memorial Day 1975, alongside Jim Bridwell and Billy Westbay. He later completed the West Face of El Cap in five hours, helping launch the modern speed climbing movement.

*Free Soloing*

Long popularized free soloing at Joshua Tree National Park during his high school years and introduced John Bachar to the practice in 1974. His 1973 ascent of Paisano Overhang established the 5.12 grade as a benchmark for difficulty.

*Big Wall Free Climbing*

In 1975, Long, Ron Kauk, and John Bachar achieved the first free ascent of Washington Column's East Face in Yosemite Valley, subsequently called Astroman—a route now considered a Yosemite classic.

*International Exploration*

Starting in 1980, Long conducted expeditions including the first coast-to-coast traverse of Borneo, discovery of the world's largest river cave in Papua New Guinea, and explorations of Angel Falls (Venezuela) and the North Pole.

**The Stonemasters**

Long joined the legendary Yosemite climbing group the Stonemasters, whose members included John Bachar and Lynn Hill. Their exploits and philosophy shaped extreme adventure sports culture in the 1970s.

**Writing and Media Career**

Long authored over forty titles spanning adventure narratives, instruction manuals, and literary works. His "Advanced Rock Climbing" won the Banff Film Festival Award. He received the 2006 Literary Award from the American Alpine Club, described as "their highest honor, rarely bestowed."

His novella "Rogue's Babylon" inspired Sylvester Stallone's film "Cliffhanger" (1993). Long worked on television productions including Emmy-nominated Red Bull Cliff Diving World Championships and produced documentaries for Discovery, History Channel, and HBO.

His large-format book "The Stonemasters: California Rock Climbers in the Seventies" won the Grand Prize at the 2010 Banff Mountain Book Festival.

In August 2009, *Rock & Ice* magazine recognized Long as "the most influential adventurer in the world" over the preceding twenty-five years, primarily due to his writing.
