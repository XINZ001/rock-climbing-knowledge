### 原文全文（英文连贯版）

On June 6, 2018, climbers Alex Honnold and Tommy Caldwell achieved a remarkable feat in Yosemite National Park by ascending El Capitan's 3,000-foot Nose route in 1 hour 58 minutes and 7 seconds—breaking the two-hour barrier for the first time in history.

**Build-up**

Honnold and Caldwell began training together on the Nose in May 2018. In May and early June, they climbed the route approximately a dozen times together, including two attempts—on May 30 (2:10:15) and June 4 (2:01:50)—that whittled the record down progressively.

**The Record Day**

Honnold described the experience: "It didn't feel that fast, but when I popped over the top I saw 1:57" and realized they'd achieved their goal. Despite dropping essential gear and having to pass other climbing parties, they shaved more than 3.5 minutes off their previous record.

**Strategy and Communication**

Honnold emphasized that "The main thing with speed climbing is to trim inefficiency." The partners consistently debriefed after each climb: "Every time we climbed the route we'd do a big debrief up top, then we'd spend most of the down hike talking about it, and then throughout the day we randomly ask each other questions, figure out ways to do things potentially better."

**Future Prospects**

Honnold suggested the theoretical human limit on this route might approach 90 minutes but indicated no immediate plans to pursue further records, predicting their achievement could stand for 10–15 years.
