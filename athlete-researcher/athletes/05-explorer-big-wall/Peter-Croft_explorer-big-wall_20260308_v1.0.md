### 原文全文（英文连贯版）

Peter Croft was born in Nanaimo, B.C., and grew up on Vancouver Island's east coast. His climbing journey began after reading Chris Bonington's "I Chose to Climb." His father introduced him to rock climbing in the Sierras, but his passion developed at a local climbing gym. He spent considerable time at the Grand Wall boulder area, learning through experience with minimal mentorship. His breakthrough came when he sent *White Lightning* (5.10) on the Apron after a year of climbing.

**University Wall (1982)**

Peter achieved the first free ascent of University Wall, a 5.12 Squamish route, alongside Foweraker and Hamish Fraser. He reflected: "I craved an odyssey that required my all–and quite possibly more…. I wanted to dive in and draw blood, and it was OK if that blood was my own."

**The Nose of El Capitan & Half Dome (1986)**

With John Bachar, Peter completed this landmark one-day link-up in just 10 hours—not to set a record, but out of practical convenience. They climbed through rain while passing seven other climbing parties. This record stood for ten years. The combination became known as the Nose in a Day (NIAD) linkup.

**Free Solo Ascents**
- The Rostrum (1985, 5.11) — First free solo
- Astroman (1987, 5.11) — First free solo
- Astroman & The Rostrum Link-up (1987) — First free solo link-up, repeated only by Dean Potter (2000) and Alex Honnold (2007)

**The Nose Speed Records**

Peter set multiple speed records on The Nose:
- 1986 with John Bachar: 10 hours (inaugural NIAD record)
- 1990 with Dave Schultz: 6:40
- 1991 with Dave Schultz: 4:48
- 1992 with Hans Florine: 4:22 (final record with Florine)

**Other Major Ascents**
- Moonlight Buttress (1991, 5.12d/13a) — First free ascent
- The Monument (1987, 5.12d) — First free ascent (onsight)
- Nose and Salathé Wall Link-up (1992, 5.13/5.13b) — First one-day link-up

**Philosophy on Free Solo**

Peter describes solo climbing as a transcendent experience, achieving a "primal state" where instinct and flow create an unmatched sensation. However, he expresses concern about modern climbers filming solos, noting that additional personnel and variables can induce stress and compromise concentration, potentially leading to mistakes or accidents.

**Legacy**

Peter is recognized in the Rock Climbing Hall of Fame for his pioneering ascents and contributions inspiring future generations. Beyond climbing, he authored climbing guidebooks including "Fifty Favorite Climbs: The Ultimate North American Tick List." As of 2024, he continues as a trail guide and climbing instructor near Bishop, California.

Peter Croft's contribution to the Nose speed record evolution:

| 年份 | 时间 | 团队 |
|------|------|------|
| 1986 | 10:00 | **Croft / Bachar** (NIAD 首次) |
| 1990 | 6:40 | **Croft / Schultz** |
| 1991 | 4:48 | **Croft / Schultz** |
| 1992 | 4:22 | **Croft / Florine** |
| 2012 | 2:23:46 | Florine / Honnold |
| 2018 | 1:58:07 | Caldwell / Honnold (current) |

Croft dominated the Nose speed record from 1986 to 1992—a six-year stretch across four successive records. His partnership with Hans Florine set the foundation for Florine's continued involvement in Nose speed records into the 2010s.
