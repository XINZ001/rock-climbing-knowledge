**Dave MacLeod** (born 17 July 1978) is a Scottish [rock climber](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rock_climber "Rock climber"), [ice climber](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ice_climbing "Ice climbing"), [mixed climber](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mixed_climbing "Mixed climbing"), and climbing author. MacLeod is known for being the [second-ever](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_grade_milestones_in_rock_climbing#Free-solo_by_men "List of grade milestones in rock climbing") person to [free solo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_solo "Free solo") a [8b+](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grade_(climbing)#Free_climbing "Grade (climbing)")(5.14a) graded route (_Darwin Dixit_ in [Margalef](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Margalef "Margalef"), in 2008),[[1]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dave_MacLeod#cite_note-DarwinDixit1-1)[[2]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dave_MacLeod#cite_note-DarwinDixit2-2) and for climbing one of the hardest [traditional climbing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Traditional_climbing "Traditional climbing") routes in the world (_[Rhapsody](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhapsody\_(climb) "Rhapsody (climb)")_ on [Dumbarton Rock](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dumbarton "Dumbarton"), graded E11 7a, in 2006).[[3]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dave_MacLeod#cite_note-Rhapsody1-3)[[4]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dave_MacLeod#cite_note-Rhapsody2-4)

North West face of [Dumbarton Rock](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dumbarton_Rock "Dumbarton Rock"). _[Rhapsody](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhapsody\_(climb) "Rhapsody (climb)")_ and _Requiem_ follow the line of the thin and tallest crack in the centre of the face. _Rhapsody_ holds the line of the crack all the way to the top, while _Requiem_ veers right.

In April 2006, MacLeod established the climb _[Rhapsody](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhapsody\_(climb) "Rhapsody (climb)")_ on [Dumbarton Rock](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dumbarton "Dumbarton") which, at a grade of [E11 7a](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grade_(climbing)#British "Grade (climbing)"), was the hardest [traditional climbing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Traditional_climbing "Traditional climbing") route in the world at the time.[[3]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dave_MacLeod#cite_note-Rhapsody1-3)[[4]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dave_MacLeod#cite_note-Rhapsody2-4) The ascent of _Rhapsody_ is the subject of the movie _E11_ (2006) directed by Paul Diffley and produced by Hot Aches Productions.[[5]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dave_MacLeod#cite_note-5)

In 2008, MacLeod became the [second-ever](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_grade_milestones_in_rock_climbing#Free-solo_by_men "List of grade milestones in rock climbing") person [free solo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_solo "Free solo") an [8b+](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grade_(climbing)#Free_climbing "Grade (climbing)")(5.14a) graded route (_Darwin Dixit_ in [Margalef](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Margalef "Margalef")).[[1]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dave_MacLeod#cite_note-DarwinDixit1-1)[[2]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dave_MacLeod#cite_note-DarwinDixit2-2)

In 2008, MacLeod completed the traditional rock climb _Echo Wall_, an extreme and as-yet ungraded rock climb on [Ben Nevis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ben_Nevis "Ben Nevis"), which took two years of preparation.[[6]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dave_MacLeod#cite_note-6) MacLeod left the route ungraded but in 2024, suggested a grade of E10 7a for the route.[[7]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dave_MacLeod#cite_note-7)

In addition to his achievements in traditional rock climbing, MacLeod has also successfully created and completed [sport climbing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sport_climbing "Sport climbing") routes and projects up to the grade of [9a](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grade_(climbing)#Free_climbing "Grade (climbing)")(5.14d) (_A Muerte_ at [Siurana](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siurana_(Tarragona) "Siurana (Tarragona)"), in 2007)[[8]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dave_MacLeod#cite_note-8) and has established [bouldering](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bouldering "Bouldering") problems up to the grade of [8B+](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grade_(climbing)#Bouldering "Grade (climbing)")(V14) (_Natural Method_ on the Skeleton Boulder at [Glen Nevis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glen_Nevis "Glen Nevis") in 2012).[[9]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dave_MacLeod#cite_note-9)

On 28 August 2010, MacLeod and [Tim Emmett](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tim_Emmett "Tim Emmett") established the route _The Usual Suspects_ on [Sron Uladail](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uladail "Uladail") on [Harris](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harris,_Outer_Hebrides "Harris, Outer Hebrides"), provisionally graded E9 7a, in an ascent broadcast live on [BBC Two Scotland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BBC_Two_Scotland "BBC Two Scotland").[[10]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dave_MacLeod#cite_note-10) As part of their preparation, MacLeod and Emmett successfully established five new routes on five Hebridean islands (counting [Lewis with Harris](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lewis_with_Harris "Lewis with Harris") as two separate islands) in five days, an achievement documented in the BBC Scotland series _5 Climbs, 5 Islands_ (later released on DVD as _Triple 5_).[[11]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dave_MacLeod#cite_note-11) MacLeod has continued his association with BBC Scotland, filming _The First Great Climb_ (broadcast on 22 November 2011), in which he replicated a successful 1876 attempt on the Stack of [Handa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Handa,_Scotland "Handa, Scotland") using the type of equipment that would have been available at the time, and _Climbing – No Limits!_ (broadcast on 12 April 2012), establishing new routes in the [Yorkshire Dales](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yorkshire_Dales "Yorkshire Dales") and the [Peak District](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peak_District "Peak District").

MacLeod has also established hard routes in [mixed climbing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mixed_climbing "Mixed climbing") with ice axes and crampons, climbing _Good Training for Something_ with Canadian climber [Will Gadd](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Will_Gadd "Will Gadd") at a grade of [M12](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grade_(climbing)#M-grade "Grade (climbing)"). In 2005, he established the hardest traditional mixed climbing route in the world at the time, _The Hurting_ in [Coire an t-Sneachda](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coire_an_t-Sneachda "Coire an t-Sneachda"), [Cairngorms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cairngorms "Cairngorms").[[12]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dave_MacLeod#cite_note-12) The route has been repeated a few times[[13]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dave_MacLeod#cite_note-13)[[14]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dave_MacLeod#cite_note-14)[[15]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dave_MacLeod#cite_note-15) and has a [Scottish winter grade](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scottish_winter_grade "Scottish winter grade") of XI,11 (M9+/M10)[[16]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dave_MacLeod#cite_note-16) with hard, technical climbing over very poor protection.

In December 2009, Macleod's book _9 Out of 10 Climbers Make the Same Mistakes: Navigation Through the Maze of Advice for the Self-coached Climber_ was published. In 2015, Macleod's book _Make or Break: Don't Let Climbing Injuries Dictate Your Success_ was published. In November 2024, Macleod's book _Moving the Needle: How an Average Climber Can Do the Hardest Route in the World_ was published. MacLeod has written widely on nutrition, and eats beef and eggs daily in the belief that animal products are essential for good mental health.[[17]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dave_MacLeod#cite_note-17)

Training with a finger injury
=============================

A few weeks ago I picked up a mild A2 pulley injury in my finger. As many of you know, I wrote a whole chapter in [Make or Break](https://www.davemacleod.com/shop/makeorbreak) about finger injuries, but I thought I would make an episode showing you how I work around it to keep training despite the finger injury. Obviously, copying exactly what I do here is not the objective - every injury is different. It's about the general principle of working around injuries and how you might go about that. I hope it comes across in the episode that finding workarounds allows you to stay in better shape and remove a lot of the psychological pain of getting injured. I’ll make another video further down the recovery process, and if you have questions, do leave a comment and I’ll try and address them.
