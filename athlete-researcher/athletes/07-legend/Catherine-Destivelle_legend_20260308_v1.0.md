**Catherine Destivelle** (born 24 July 1960) is a French [rock climber](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rock_climbing "Rock climbing") and [mountaineer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mountaineering "Mountaineering") who is considered one of the greatest and most important climbers in the [history of the sport](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_rock_climbing "History of rock climbing"). She came to prominence in the mid-1980s for [sport climbing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sport_climbing "Sport climbing") by winning the first major female [climbing competitions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climbing_competition "Climbing competition"), and by being the [first woman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_first_ascents_(sport_climbing)#Redpointed_by_women "List of first ascents (sport climbing)") to [redpoint](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Redpoint_(climbing) "Redpoint (climbing)") a [7c+/8a](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grade_(climbing)#Free_climbing "Grade (climbing)") sport climbing route with _Fleur de Rocaille_ in 1985,[[a]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-8a-2) and an [8a+](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grade_(climbing)#Free_climbing "Grade (climbing)")(5.13c) route with _Choucas_ in 1988. During this period, she was considered the strongest female sport climber in the world along with [Lynn Hill](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lynn_Hill "Lynn Hill"); however, in 1990 she retired to focus on [alpine climbing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alpine_climbing "Alpine climbing").

In 1990, she made the first female alpine ascent of the _Bonatti Pillar_ on the [Petit Dru](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aiguille_du_Dru "Aiguille du Dru"), which she followed up in 1991, by becoming the first woman to create a new extreme alpine route, also on the Petit Dru, which was named _Voie Destivelle_ in her honor. From 1992 to 1994, Destivelle became the first female to complete the winter alpine [free solo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_soloing "Free soloing") of the "[north face trilogy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_north_faces_of_the_Alps "Great north faces of the Alps")" of the [Eiger](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eiger "Eiger"), the [Grandes Jorasses](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grandes_Jorasses "Grandes Jorasses"), and the [Matterhorn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Matterhorn "Matterhorn"). She made Himalayan and high-altitude ascents such as [Nameless Tower](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trango_Towers "Trango Towers") in 1990, the southwest face of [Shishapangma](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shishapangma "Shishapangma") in 1995, and the south face of [Peak 4111](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peak_4111 "Peak 4111"), in Antarctica, in 1996.

As well as her Alpine free solos, she made other notable free solos, such as the [Devils Tower](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Devils_Tower "Devils Tower") in 1992, and the [Old Man of Hoy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_Man_of_Hoy "Old Man of Hoy") in 1997. She is the subject of several documentaries, including Rémy Tezier's, _Beyond the Summits_, which won the best feature-length film award at the 2009 [Banff Film Festival](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Banff_Mountain_Film_Festival "Banff Mountain Film Festival"). In 2007, she was made a Knight of the [Legion of Honour](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legion_of_Honour "Legion of Honour"), and in 2020, became the first female recipient of the [Piolet d'Or](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Piolet_d%27Or "Piolet d'Or")_Lifetime Achievement Award_.

Early life and education
------------------------

Catherine Destivelle was born in [Oran](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oran_(department) "Oran (department)"), in [French Algeria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Algeria "French Algeria"), to French parents, Serge and Annie Destivelle. Catherine was the eldest of six: four sisters Florence, Sophie, Martine, and Claire, and one brother [Hyacinthe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hyacinthe_Destivelle "Hyacinthe Destivelle") (born Laurent). Her father was an amateur climber and mountaineer. As a young teenager, her family moved to Paris, France, where she attended the Lycée Corot in [Savigny-sur-Orge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Savigny-sur-Orge "Savigny-sur-Orge").[[2]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-M1-3)[[3]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-BIO-4)

At the age of 12, Destivelle became a member of the [Club alpin français](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Club_alpin_fran%C3%A7ais "Club alpin français"), and started [bouldering](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bouldering "Bouldering") in [Fontainebleau](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fontainebleau_rock_climbing "Fontainebleau rock climbing"),[[4]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-Faces:_Catherine_Destivelle-5) multi-pitch [big wall climbing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Big_wall_climbing "Big wall climbing") in [Burgundy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saffres "Saffres"), and alpine climbing in the [Massif des Écrins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Massif_des_%C3%89crins "Massif des Écrins").[[2]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-M1-3) By 1976, aged 16, she was spending her summer in the [Verdon Gorge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Verdon_Gorge "Verdon Gorge"), with climbing-partner, Pierre Richard. In that year, Destiville made alpine ascents of the _Cousy-Desmaison Route_ ([ED](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grade_(climbing)#International_French_Adjectival_System "Grade (climbing)")) on the north face of [l'Olan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olan_(mountain) "Olan (mountain)"), and the _Devies-Gervasutti Route_ ([TD+](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grade_(climbing)#International_French_Adjectival_System "Grade (climbing)")), on the northwest face of [Ailefroide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ailefroide "Ailefroide").[[4]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-Faces:_Catherine_Destivelle-5) The following year, in 1977, she ascended the _American Direct Route_ ([ED1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grade_(climbing)#International_French_Adjectival_System "Grade (climbing)")), on the west face of [Le Petit Dru](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aiguille_du_Dru "Aiguille du Dru") in [Chamonix](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chamonix "Chamonix").[[4]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-Faces:_Catherine_Destivelle-5)

From 1980 to 1985, Destivelle focused on studying [physiotherapy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physiotherapy "Physiotherapy") at the _Ecole de kinésithérapie de Paris_, and then working full-time as a physiotherapist.[[3]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-BIO-4)[[4]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-Faces:_Catherine_Destivelle-5)[[5]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-PM5-6)

### Sport climbing and competition climbing

In 1985, Destivelle became a professional climber, which happened almost by accident after being asked to do a climbing film, _E pericoloso sporgersi_, that captured her making the first female ascent of the 1,000-foot multi-pitch sport route _Pichenibule_ in [Verdon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Verdon_Gorge "Verdon Gorge"), which was only the second-ever female ascent in history of a [7b](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grade_(climbing)#Free_climbing "Grade (climbing)")(5.12b) route;[[2]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-M1-3)[[4]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-Faces:_Catherine_Destivelle-5) after the film, she was offered sponsorships.[[3]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-BIO-4)[[6]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-BMC-7) In 1985, Destivelle made what was thought to be the [first female](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_first_ascents_(sport_climbing)#Redpointed_by_women "List of first ascents (sport climbing)") breakthrough into the [8a](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grade_(climbing)#Free_climbing "Grade (climbing)")(5.13b) grade with _Fleur de Rocaille_, however, its grade was subsequently softened to 7c+/8a.[[a]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-8a-2)[[2]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-M1-3)

Destivelle initially rejected [competition climbing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Competition_climbing "Competition climbing"), co-signing the 1985 [Manifeste des 19](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Manifeste_des_19&action=edit&redlink=1 "Manifeste des 19 (page does not exist)")[[fr](https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manifeste_des_19 "fr:Manifeste des 19")], but then changed her mind and won at [Sportroccia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sportroccia "Sportroccia") in 1985, the first international climbing competition (held in [Bardonecchia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bardonecchia "Bardonecchia") and [Arco](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arco,_Trentino "Arco, Trentino")), which later became the [Rock Master](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rock_Master "Rock Master") annual competition.[[4]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-Faces:_Catherine_Destivelle-5) Later that year, she fractured her pelvis in a fall in Chamonix.[[2]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-M1-3)[[4]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-Faces:_Catherine_Destivelle-5) Destivelle recovered and in 1986 set new records by becoming the first female to climb an [8a](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grade_(climbing)#Free_climbing "Grade (climbing)")(5.13b) route with _Fleur de Rocaille_ (later downgraded to 7c+/8a), and winning again at Sportroccia, beating her main rival [Lynn Hill](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lynn_Hill "Lynn Hill").[[2]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-M1-3)[[5]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-PM5-6)[[8]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-9)

1988 would be the pinnacle of Destivelle's sport climbing career when she redpointed _Choucas_ in [Buoux](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buoux "Buoux"), the [first female](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_first_ascents_(sport_climbing)#Redpointed_by_women "List of first ascents (sport climbing)") ascent of an [8a+](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grade_(climbing)#Free_climbing "Grade (climbing)")(5.13c) graded route.[[4]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-Faces:_Catherine_Destivelle-5)[[9]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-RILOT-10) She had repeated several [8a](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grade_(climbing)#Free_climbing "Grade (climbing)")(5.13b) routes that year in preparation, including _Rêve de Papillon_, _Elixir de Violence_, _Samizdat_, and _La Diagonale du Fou_.[[3]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-BIO-4) She also beat her fellow French rival [Isabelle Patissier](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isabelle_Patissier "Isabelle Patissier") to win her third Sportroccia title.[[2]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-M1-3)[[4]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-Faces:_Catherine_Destivelle-5) In 1989, Destivelle won the first international climbing competition held in the US, organized by [Jeff Lowe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jeff_Lowe "Jeff Lowe") at [Snowbird, Utah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Snowbird,_Utah "Snowbird, Utah").[[2]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-M1-3)[[4]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-Faces:_Catherine_Destivelle-5)[[10]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-11) In 1990, Destivelle finished third at the annual Snowbird international competition and decided to retire from competition climbing to focus on mountaineering and alpine climbing.[[4]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-Faces:_Catherine_Destivelle-5)

In 1990, Destivelle came to international attention[[11]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-CLOT-12) with the first female ascent of the _Bonatti Pillar_ (TD+: 5.9 A1) on the southwest face of the Petit Dru, which she completed as a [free solo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_solo "Free solo") in 4 hours; in 1955 [Walter Bonatti](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walter_Bonatti "Walter Bonatti") spent six days on the route as he made what became regarded as one of the most famous ascents in [history](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_rock_climbing#1950s "History of rock climbing").[[4]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-Faces:_Catherine_Destivelle-5)[[12]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-M2-13)

In 1991, Destivelle completed one of her most notable alpine climbing feats by opening up a new route on the west face of the Petit Dru, named the _Voie Destivelle_ (or _Destiville Route_) (VI 5.11b A5). Her 11-day free solo of the route, which bears her name in her honor, was captured in the film _11 Days on the Dru_, and covered widely in the international media.[[4]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-Faces:_Catherine_Destivelle-5)[[12]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-M2-13)

Destivelle turned her attention to completing the free solo climb, in winter, of the three greatest north faces in the Alpes (the winter "[North Face Trilogy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_north_faces_of_the_Alps "Great north faces of the Alps")" of [Ivano Ghirardini](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ivano_Ghirardini "Ivano Ghirardini")).[[13]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-PMLOT-14) She started in 1992, making the [first female solo ascent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_climbing_the_Eiger#1990s "Timeline of climbing the Eiger") of the _1938 Heckmair Route_ (ED2) on the north face of the [Eiger](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eiger "Eiger") in 17-hours (and featured in the 1992 climbing film, _Eiger_).[[4]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-Faces:_Catherine_Destivelle-5)[[13]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-PMLOT-14) In 1993, she made the first female solo ascent of the _Walker Spur_ (ED1, 5.8 A1) on the north face of the [Grandes Jorasses](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grandes_Jorasses "Grandes Jorasses").[[4]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-Faces:_Catherine_Destivelle-5)[[13]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-PMLOT-14) In 1994, she completed the 'winter solo trilogy' [climbing the](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_climbing_the_Matterhorn#1994 "Timeline of climbing the Matterhorn")_Bonatti Route_ (ED2/3) on the [Matterhorn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Matterhorn "Matterhorn").[[4]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-Faces:_Catherine_Destivelle-5)[[12]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-M2-13)[[13]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-PMLOT-14)

In 1999, she completed the first female free solo of the _Brandler-Hasse Route_ (ED-: 5.10c A0), on the north face direct of the [Cima Grande di Lavaredo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cima_Grande_di_Lavaredo "Cima Grande di Lavaredo"); her last major alpine climb.[[4]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-Faces:_Catherine_Destivelle-5)

### Himalayan and high-altitude climbing

In 1990, Destivelle went on an expedition with Jeff Lowe and [David Breashears](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Breashears "David Breashears") to try a new route on [Nameless Tower](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trango_Towers#Trango_(Nameless)_Tower "Trango Towers") in the [Karakoram](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Karakoram "Karakoram") in [Pakistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pakistan "Pakistan"), however, poor conditions forced them to change plans and they instead made the second free ascent of _Yugoslav Route_ (VI 5.12a) (and featured in the 1990 climbing film, _Nameless Tower_).[[12]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-M2-13)[[14]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-aaj_1991-15) In 1992, Destivelle went on another expedition with Jeff Lowe to climb the north ridge on the north face of [Latok I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latok "Latok"), in the [Karakoram](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Karakoram "Karakoram"), but was forced back due to severe storms.[[15]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-aaj_1993-16)

In 1993, she went on an expedition to the west face of [Makalu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Makalu "Makalu") with [Jeff Lowe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jeff_Lowe "Jeff Lowe") and French mountaineering guide, Erik Decamp (who would later become her husband). Lowe tried an alternative solo route while Destivelle and Decamp tried the West Pillar, however, both groups were unsuccessful due to severe snowfall.[[16]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-AAJM-17) In 1995, she and Decamp made a successful ascent of the _Loretan-Troillet-Kurtyka Route_ on the southwest face of [Shishapangma](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shishapangma "Shishapangma").[[17]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-AAJ3-18) In 1995, the pair were less successful in attempting to open a new route on the south face of [Annapurna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annapurna "Annapurna"), beside the old _Bonington Route_.[[18]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-AAJ4-19) In January 1996, after the pair successfully completed a new route on the south face of [Peak 4111](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peak_4111 "Peak 4111") in Antarctica, Destivelle fell 20-metres through a cornice while momentarily unroped on the summit, and suffered a severe compound leg fracture. Destivelle thought she would die given their remote position, however, a 15-hour self-rescue brought her to safety.[[19]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-AAJ5-20)

Destivelle is known for her free soloing of multi-pitch rock climbs, and both alpine big wall and alpine mixed climbs (i.e. rock climbing and ice climbing).[[20]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-CFS-21)[[21]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-22) On a 1992 tour to the US, she free soloed the second half of _El Matador_[5.10d](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grade_(climbing)#Free_climbing "Grade (climbing)")(6b+) on the [Devils Tower](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Devils_Tower "Devils Tower") in Wyoming, and _Supercrack_[5.10b](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grade_(climbing)#Free_climbing "Grade (climbing)")(6a+), in [Indian Creek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_Creek_(climbing_area) "Indian Creek (climbing area)"), Utah (both are captured in the 1992 climbing film, _Ballade à Devil's Tower_), and in 1997, while four months pregnant, free soloed the [Old Man of Hoy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_Man_of_Hoy "Old Man of Hoy") in Scotland (captured in the 1998 film, _Rock Queen_).[[20]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-CFS-21)

During 1992–1994, she completed the winter free solo trilogy of the north faces of the Eiger, the Grandes Jorasses and the Matterhorn.[[20]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-CFS-21) In a 2020 interview with _[Rock & Ice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rock\_%26\_Ice "Rock & Ice")_ she said: "When I'm free soloing, I feel O.K. I always have a big safety margin, I'm not struggling. You feel quite powerful and calm. If I ever felt afraid, I wouldn't go. I don't like to bet".[[20]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-CFS-21)[[22]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-RI1-23) She said of her Eiger free solo: "I didn't want people to say it was the first female ascent, I wanted to be the first person to climb the Eiger onsight and solo in winter".[[22]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-RI1-23)

Destivelle is widely considered one of the greatest all-around female climbers in the history of the sport.[[6]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-BMC-7)[[5]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-PM5-6)[[11]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-CLOT-12)[[23]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-Gripped10-24) In 2014, the former editor of the _[Alpine Journal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alpine\_Journal "Alpine Journal")_, Ed Douglas, called her "the world's most famous woman alpinist during the 1990s".[[6]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-BMC-7) When she became the first female recipient of the Piolet d'Or _Lifetime Achievement Award_ in 2020, _PlanetMountain_ said: "the 59-year-old Frenchwoman is considered one of the greatest climbers and mountaineers of all times".[[13]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-PMLOT-14) In 2020, _[Climbing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climbing\_(magazine) "Climbing (magazine)")_ magazine noted her pioneering role in competition climbing and her rivalry with Lynn Hill as they vied for the title of the strongest female sport climber, but noted that her true passion was for alpinism, and said of her achievements: "Catherine Destivelle crushed the stereotypes that top-level sport climbing and daring alpinism were reserved for men. She is one of the most well-rounded climbers of all time—from the boulders of Fontainebleau to the Himalaya—and her storied career serves as inspiration to climbers everywhere".[[11]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-CLOT-12)

In 1996, Destivelle and Erik Decamp were married, and have a son.[[4]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-Faces:_Catherine_Destivelle-5)[[24]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-Indep-25) After her son was born, Destivelle began to cut back on free solo and extreme climbs in the late 1990s and developed a career as a lecturer, speaker and a mountaineering writer. By 2000, she completely retired from any major sport climbing or alpine climbing activities.[[24]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-Indep-25)

In 2011, in partnership with Bruno Dupety, she became a publisher at her firm, "Les Editions du Mont Blanc", a company specializing in books about mountaineering and alpinism.[[25]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-26)

*   1992 – _Supercrack_[5.10b](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grade_(climbing)#Free_climbing "Grade (climbing)")(6a+), [Indian Creek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_Creek_(climbing_area) "Indian Creek (climbing area)"), [Dead Horse Point State Park](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dead_Horse_Point_State_Park "Dead Horse Point State Park") in Utah (captured in the 1992 climbing film, _Ballade à Devil's Tower_).[[30]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Destivelle#cite_note-31)

### 原文全文（英文）

```text
Catherine Destivelle was born and raised in Paris and was introduced to climbing at a young age in the Forêt de Fontainebleau on the outskirts of the city. By the time she was 15 she could climb the toughest rocks. At 17 she was spending her weekends scaling the highest peaks in the Alps. Around the age of 20, she settled down to a career as a physiotherapist, but after five years the lure of the mountains proved too much and she started competing in international climbing competitions. From 1985 to 1988 she was considered the world’s best woman climber. In 1990 she made her mountaineering comeback with a series of incredible climbs, including solo winter ascents of the three most legendary Alpine walls – the Eiger, the Grandes Jorasses and the Matterhorn – making her the outstanding female climber of all time. In 2020, Catherine Destivelle was awarded the Piolet d’Or Carrière for her lifetime achievements and contributions to the world of mountaineering. She is the first woman to be honoured with this award.

NOTABLE ACHIEVEMENTS

*    In 1990 she free-climbed with Jeff Lowe the Yougouslaves route on the Nameless Tower at Trango Towers in the Karakoram, Pakistan and then solo climbed the Bonatti Pillar at Les Drus in 4 hours.
*    June 1991, she opened a new route up the famous west face of the Drus, during a remarkable 11-day solo-climb.
*    August 1992, attempt with Jeff Lowe of the North ridge of Latok I (Karakoram, Pakistan), a route still unbeaten to this day.
*   In March 1992, she climbed the legendary north face of the Eiger solo in winter in 17 hours via the Heckmair route, without any prior scouting (on sight). In summer 1993, in the company of Erik Decamp, she attempted an alpine-style ascent of the west pillar of Makalu (8481 metres).
*    March 1994, first repetition of the Bonatti route on the north face of the Matterhorn, a winter solo ascent; In 1994, with Erik Decamp, repeat of the Kurtyka-Loretan-Troillet route on the south face of Shishapangma, 8013 metres, in Tibet.
*    In 1996, first ascent with Érik Decamp of the "unnamed" Peak 4111 (4,160 m) in the Ellsworth Range in Antarctica. An accident forced her to return early from this expedition.
*    In June 1999, Catherine made a solo ascent of the Brandler-Hasse route on the north face of Cima Grande in the Dolomites. Catherine was once again the first woman to make this solo ascent.
*    She is still to this day the only woman to have soloed the Grandes Jorasses, the Matterhorn and the north face of the Eiger.
```

### 原文全文（英文）

```text
Published July 21, 2021 05:00AM

**I did the _American Direct_(ED1 5.10+ A0, 1,100m)**on the Dru when I was 17. I was just an amateur. I was climbing because I liked climbing, but I didn’t know I would be able to do what I did after. It was just pleasure. I was still a child.

**When Lynn Hill came [to Europe to compete]**, I was scared. Everybody told me, “Lynn is very strong.” I didn’t compete a lot—one competition each year. Lynn was doing more. The media built up the rivalry between us. It’s not as real during the competition. Lynn was my best rival. I won some, she won some. She was very clear and focused. Very good spirit.

**When I’m free soloing,**I feel O.K. I always have a big safety margin, I’m not struggling. You feel quite powerful and calm. If I ever felt afraid, I wouldn’t go. I don’t like to bet. That goes for everything. I don’t run after luck. With my publishing company, it’s exactly the same. I go step by step and build something.

**[On her solo FA on the Petit Dru in 1991]**I realized I don’t like aid climbing. It was boring. But I learned a lot about myself: [It was] long, four days of bad weather, couldn’t go out of my portaledge. The climb lasted 11 days—too long! I felt very embarrassed because I had a lot of press and media. I didn’t feel comfortable with that.

**[On the helicopter ride down**off the Dru]I felt weak accepting that, but when I was on the top, my friends were saying, ‘Oh, just take it.’ …It ruined the climb at the end for me, kind of. Even if other people don’t know I took the helicopter,_I_ know I took it. … It was this feeling of leaving something unfinished.

I never did something like that again.

**When I soloed the North Face**of the Eiger in one day in 1992, my goal was to do it without knowing the route, onsight. I didn’t want people to say it was the first female ascent, I wanted to be the first _person_ to climb the Eiger onsight and solo in winter (though I don’t know for sure that I was!).

**[On a 20-meter fall in 1985**at the summit of Peak 4111, Ellsworth Mountains, Antarctica, just after the first ascent, with Eric Decamp]I was taking photos and I lost my balance. A stupid accident. I landed with an open fracture. We were only two. At first I thought,_I’m going to die._ We were more than 4,000 meters high, far away from everyone, no rescue possible. But then we started to go down slowly. It took 16 hours.

I learned that I was too confident …. I unroped on top and I thought I was safe.

**Concentration is the biggest**thing I learned through my career. You can’t succeed if you are not focused. Climbing taught me that. You never let it go. Concentration in each instant, each minute, each second.

Concentration is an important thing in life. Without it, you do less.

**A guy called me and told me I**should do a book with him. … I thought it would be nice to share my knowledge. So I built up my publishing company. Now I have 58 books. A lot of them are translations. … I’m also publishing books for kids, because there’s nothing out there for kids. One of them is called _L’escalade, Tu Connais?_

**Being a mother is exactly** the same as climbing. Never let go. I focus on my child. Because I want his life to be good, not mine. You have to desire things for them, but not too much. I do my best for him to be happy and do what he likes—so he doesn’t climb! He likes sailing boats.

**When I can, I still climb.** I went climbing yesterday and the day before. I still love it.
```
