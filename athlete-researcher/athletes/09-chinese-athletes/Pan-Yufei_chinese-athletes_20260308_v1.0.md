**Background**

Pan Yufei (潘愚非) was born on June 23, 2000, in Hubei Province. He joined the Chinese national climbing team in 2015 at age 15, making him one of the earliest members of China's post-Olympic-announcement climbing generation. As of 2025, he is the only Chinese climber to have competed in two consecutive Olympic Games.

**Tokyo 2020 Olympics (2021) — First Olympic Appearance**

Pan Yufei represented China at the 2021 Tokyo Olympics — the first time climbing was included in the Olympic program. He competed in the men's combined event (speed + bouldering + lead) and finished 14th. The experience was groundbreaking for China's climbing program, offering Pan his first exposure to the Olympic competition environment.

**Paris 2024 Olympics — Second Appearance**

At the 2024 Paris Games, Pan Yufei competed in the men's combined event (bouldering + lead, no longer including speed, which was now a separate discipline). He placed 12th in the semifinal, again narrowly missing the final cutoff. Despite the result, the back-to-back Olympic appearances reflected the trust the national team placed in his experience and consistency.

**Philosophy: Simplicity and Presence**

Pan Yufei's stated competitive philosophy is "心无旁骛，与岩壁对话" — "Undistracted, in dialogue with the wall." He approaches each climb as an interaction between himself and the rock, stripping away external noise. His broader sporting philosophy is captured in the phrase "享受攀爬" — "Enjoy climbing" — reflecting a belief that the intrinsic experience of climbing should underpin competitive motivation, rather than external validation.

**Difficult Years: 2021–2024**

Between Tokyo and Paris, Pan Yufei went through a difficult period where results were inconsistent and he questioned his own suitability for competition. He was candid about this in post-2025 interviews: "The past few years have been really hard for me. Many times I felt like I wasn't suited for competition."

**2025 Breakthrough: China's First Bouldering World Cup Champion**

On June 15, 2025, Pan Yufei won the bouldering gold medal at the IFSC Climbing World Cup, becoming the first Chinese athlete ever to win an IFSC World Cup event in the bouldering discipline — a historic milestone. He completed three of the four final routes to win with a total score of 84.2 points, edging the French athlete by 0.1. He was reportedly competing through an injury.

His reaction after winning: "I feel like none of this is real. My mind is completely blank right now. The past few years have been really difficult for me. Many times I didn't think I was suited for competition." The win marked a transformational moment in his career and for Chinese bouldering.

**Climbing Philosophy**

Pan Yufei repeatedly returns to the idea that climbing must be emotionally authentic to be sustainable. "Enjoy climbing" is not a platitude for him but a survival principle — the mechanism that kept him training through years of inconsistent results and self-doubt. His 2025 World Cup victory validated this patient, intrinsically motivated approach.
