**Background**

Deng Lijuan (邓丽娟) was born in 2000 and is currently 24 years old. She studies at Donghua University (东华大学) in Shanghai. She began climbing at age 15 in 2015, having previously been a track athlete. Deng made the cross-discipline transition and within nine years rose to become one of China's — and the world's — fastest speed climbers.

**A Climber Born from Track**

Deng Lijuan's origins in track and field gave her an unusual athletic foundation for speed climbing: explosive lower-body power, fast neuromuscular activation, and a runner's sense of rhythm. After switching to speed climbing at 15, she developed rapidly through China's national training system. Her coaching structure and physical baseline proved highly compatible with the demands of the 15-meter standardized speed wall.

**World Cup Record Before Paris**

Prior to the 2024 Paris Olympics, Deng Lijuan had accumulated a strong international record: 5 gold medals and 3 bronze medals in IFSC World Cup speed events. This track record confirmed she was not merely a domestic champion but a genuine world-class competitor.

**Paris 2024 Olympics — Historic Silver Medal**

On August 7, 2024, at the Paris Olympics, Deng Lijuan competed in the women's speed final against Poland's Aleksandra Miroslaw — the reigning world record holder. Deng posted a time of 6.18 seconds, setting a new Asian record. Miroslaw won with 6.10 seconds; the margin was 0.08 seconds.

Deng Lijuan's silver medal was the first Olympic climbing medal in China's history. This historic breakthrough came in the sport's first Paris Olympics cycle as an expanded event with separate speed, bouldering, and lead disciplines — giving China its best chance to compete in speed, where years of specialized development had paid off.

**Significance of the Medal**

The medal was described by Chinese media as the culmination of "20 years of quiet effort" by the Chinese climbing community. Behind Deng Lijuan's 6.18 seconds were decades of systematic development: cross-discipline athlete recruitment, national training camps, and the construction of standardized speed walls across the country. Chinese climbing coaches acknowledged that AI-assisted video analysis tools had been part of the 2024 training preparation, analyzing movement data to refine Deng's technique.

**Personal Philosophy**

Deng Lijuan's guiding principle is expressed in a simple statement that she has repeated in interviews: "努力！你才有可能成功" — "Work hard! Only then do you have a chance to succeed." She described her transition into climbing as natural once she found how well her track background translated to the vertical wall.

**AI-Assisted Preparation**

Reports emerged after the Paris Games that China's climbing team had incorporated AI-assisted video analysis tools into preparation for the Paris Olympics. These tools analyzed movement sequences on the speed wall, identifying micro-inefficiencies in technique that are difficult to detect by human eye alone. The technology was part of a broader push by the Chinese national sports system to use data science in athletic preparation.

**Cross-Discipline Recruitment Model**

The success of Deng Lijuan — a former track athlete — reinforced the validity of China's 2017 cross-discipline selection initiative. In 2017, the national sports authority recruited 3,716 candidates from other athletic disciplines (martial arts, track, gymnastics, swimming) to evaluate for suitability in Olympic-new sports including climbing, surfing, skateboarding, and BMX. Of those, 440 were admitted to national training camps. Deng Lijuan's success demonstrates the model's effectiveness when athletes with strong physical foundations are matched to speed climbing's specific demands.

**National Speed Climbing Infrastructure**

Behind Deng's performance is a national infrastructure that grew significantly after climbing entered the Olympic program. China's national training system includes standardized 15-meter speed walls at multiple training bases, enabling year-round competition-level preparation. The national team's coaching staff includes specialists who analyze each technique component — grip, footwork, body positioning, arm drive — with the same rigor applied to sprinting.
