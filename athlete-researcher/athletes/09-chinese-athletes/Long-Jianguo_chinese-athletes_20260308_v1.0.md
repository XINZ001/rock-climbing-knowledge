**Background**

Long Jianguo (龙见国) was born in 2003 in Qiandongnan (黔东南), Guizhou Province. A specialist in speed climbing, he emerged from Guizhou's provincial training system and rose rapidly through the national ranks during the early 2020s. His trajectory mirrors that of the broader Chinese speed climbing program's accelerated post-2017 development phase.

**2023 National Record: Tying the World Record at 5.00 Seconds**

On March 19, 2023, at the National Climbing Championships in China, Long Jianguo set a new national record in men's speed climbing with a time of 5.00 seconds — tying the then-current IFSC world record. Because the event was a domestic national championship rather than an officially sanctioned IFSC competition, the time was not eligible for ratification as an official world record, but it confirmed Long as one of the fastest humans ever to climb the standardized 15-meter wall.

The 5.00-second performance was celebrated in Chinese climbing circles as a generational leap — evidence that China's speed climbing talent depth had grown far beyond the era when only Zhong Qixin stood at the world level.

**Ambition: The 4.90-Second Barrier**

In a November 2023 profile interview, Long Jianguo stated his target: "I want to climb to the Olympic podium, and I want to break 4.90 seconds." At the time, the world record was approaching 4.90. His stated ambition represented alignment between his personal technical goals and international competition requirements. The 4.90 threshold — sub-five by a full tenth — was the target he saw as necessary to contend for world gold.

**2025 Seoul World Championships — Historic Gold**

On September 25, 2025, at the IFSC Climbing World Championships in Seoul, South Korea, Long Jianguo won the men's speed climbing gold medal with a final time of 4.80 seconds, defeating Germany's Leander Cammans. This made Long Jianguo the first Chinese male athlete to win the World Championship title in speed climbing since Zhong Qixin's 2012 victory in Paris — a gap of exactly 13 years.

The victory was immediately recognized as historic: the Chinese media described it as "继承衣钵" (inheriting the legacy) of the Zhong Qixin era, with Long Jianguo representing the next chapter of China's world-class speed climbing tradition. Chinese Olympic officials noted the result also secured qualification points for the 2026 Asian Games.

**Time Progression**

Long Jianguo's development arc illustrates an athlete who progressed from national record holder to world champion over approximately two years — one of the steepest ascent trajectories in contemporary speed climbing. His 2023 time of 5.00 seconds and his 2025 championship time of 4.80 seconds represent a full 0.20-second improvement at elite level, indicating continued technical refinement beyond raw physical development.

**Significance for Chinese Speed Climbing**

Long Jianguo's 2025 gold arrived 13 years after Zhong Qixin's last world title and confirmed that China's post-2017 investment in cross-discipline athlete recruitment and speed climbing infrastructure had produced a second generation of world-class performers. Together with Wu Peng's Paris silver and Deng Lijuan's historic Olympic medal, Long's world championship completed China's emergence as a systemic speed climbing power rather than a one-person phenomenon.
