**Early Life and Prodigious Talent**

Zhang Yuetong (张悦彤) was born in 2003 in Shenzhen, Guangdong Province. Her introduction to climbing began at age 4 — making her one of the youngest children to begin systematic climbing anywhere in China. By age 9, she had won her provincial championship. By 12, she was national champion. At 13, she became the youngest athlete ever selected into the Chinese national training squad. These milestones established Zhang as one of the most naturally gifted climbers of her generation.

**Setback: Triple Finger Fractures in 2019**

In 2019, Zhang Yuetong suffered a serious setback when she fractured three fingers — a particularly devastating injury for a competition climber dependent on precise finger strength and contact. The rehabilitation required patience and careful rebuilding of training load. Despite this disruption during a key developmental phase, Zhang returned to competition and eventually qualified for the Paris Olympics.

**2023 Asian Qualifier Victory**

On November 11, 2023, in Jakarta, Indonesia, Zhang Yuetong won the 2023 IFSC Asian Olympic Qualifier for the women's combined discipline (bouldering + lead). She posted a total score of 142.3 points to claim first place, securing China's quota spot for the 2024 Paris Olympics in the combined event. This was a significant competitive validation coming less than four years after her finger injury.

**Training System**

In preparation for Paris, Zhang Yuetong trained under Japanese coach Ito Hidekazu, a technical specialist. Her coaching team identified lead climbing as her primary strength and bouldering as her relative weakness — a gap that would become relevant in the Olympic combined format. Her training incorporated systematic sessions across both disciplines alongside regular competition exposure.

**Paris 2024 Olympics**

At the Paris Games, Zhang Yuetong competed in the women's combined event (bouldering + lead semifinal). In the bouldering portion, she scored well and was placed around 9th. In the lead portion, she posted 68 points from 4 routes, ranking 4th individually in that segment. Her combined total of 99.7 points placed her 13th overall — short of the final cutoff.

In her post-competition comments, Zhang noted that the lead route in Paris was "simpler than expected," which reduced her ability to build a decisive advantage in her stronger discipline. She described the experience as both regrettable and educational: "I have regrets, but also gains."

**Philosophy and Identity**

Zhang Yuetong approaches competition with a fluid, expressive style that media have described as "ballet on the cliff face" — 峭壁上的芭蕾. Her stated competitive philosophy is: "就放开爬就好了！" — "Just climb freely!" and "做好自己，其他的交给天意" — "Do your best; leave the rest to fate." This outlook reflects her belief in process over outcome — a notable quality for an athlete who has already experienced significant highs and a serious injury before the age of 21.
