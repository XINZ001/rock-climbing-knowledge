**Background and Early Career**

Wu Peng (伍鹏) was born in 2002 in Shaoyang, Hunan Province, China. A specialist in speed climbing, he rose through the Chinese national team's training system to become one of the fastest speed climbers in the world. By the lead-up to the 2024 Paris Olympics, he had established himself as a genuine medal contender.

**Breakthrough: 2024 Wujiang World Cup**

Wu Peng's coming-out moment on the international stage arrived at the 2024 IFSC Climbing World Cup in Wujiang (April 2024). This victory signaled that Wu had fully matured from prospect to elite competitor. Reflecting on that performance, he stated: "Stable performance comes from the constant accumulation of training. I didn't think about how fast to climb — I just focused on letting my training-level performance come through."

**Paris 2024 Olympics — Quarter-final, Semifinal, and Final**

At the 2024 Paris Olympics, Wu Peng advanced through an intense bracket. In the quarter-final, he won by a margin of just 0.002 seconds over the Italian competitor. In the semifinal, he eliminated his main rival Watson to advance to the gold medal match.

In the final against Indonesia's Leonardo, both climbers produced extraordinary runs. Leonardo claimed gold with 4.75 seconds; Wu Peng posted 4.77 seconds — a personal best — to win the silver medal. He fell short by 0.02 seconds.

Wu Peng became the first Chinese male climber to win an Olympic medal in the speed event at the Paris Games alongside Deng Lijuan's silver in the women's event.

**Post-Race Reaction**

In his immediate post-race interview, Wu Peng was candid: "I'm a bit happy, but there's a lot of regret. I think I already gave everything I had. I was just that little bit short. I'm a little unconvinced." He later elaborated that he felt he had produced his best-ever performance and simply faced a better athlete on that day.

**Pre-Race Mental State and Ritual**

Wu Peng described his mental preparation before competition as entering a state of complete self-enclosure: "When I return to the isolation zone, I put on my headphones. I don't know what others are saying or doing." This deliberate sensory isolation — blocking out all external stimulus — allowed him to maintain full concentration before stepping onto the wall.
