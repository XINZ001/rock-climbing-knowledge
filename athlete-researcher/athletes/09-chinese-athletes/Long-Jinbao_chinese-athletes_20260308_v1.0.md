**Background: From Martial Arts to Climbing**

Long Jinbao (龙金宝) was born in Fenghuang (凤凰), a small city in western Hunan Province (湘西). His athletic career began not with climbing but with sanda — Chinese kickboxing (散打). In April 2017, he was selected to travel to Changsha, Hunan's capital, where he was introduced to climbing for the first time. The physical attributes developed through martial arts training — explosive power, body coordination, mental discipline — transferred well to speed climbing's demands. Long Jinbao took to the vertical wall quickly.

He is enrolled at Hunan Normal University (湖南师范大学) in the School of Physical Education's 2021 elite athlete class (健将班).

**Progression and 2023 World Championship**

Long Jinbao's rapid development culminated at the 2023 IFSC Climbing World Championships, where he competed in the men's speed final. In the semifinal, he improved his personal best from 5.05 seconds to 5.03 seconds. In the final against Italy's Matteo Zurloni, he claimed the silver medal — and simultaneously secured China's quota spot for the 2024 Paris Olympics in the men's speed event. This was a landmark result: a top-two finish at the World Championships by a Chinese male speed climber.

**2023 Asian Games (Hangzhou)**

At the 2023 Hangzhou Asian Games, Long Jinbao earned two medals: silver in the individual men's speed event, and gold in the speed relay — contributing to China's relay dominance in Asia. He also won the inaugural National Climbing Championship in 2023 in the individual speed event and in relay.

**Paris 2024 Olympics**

At the 2024 Paris Olympics, Long Jinbao competed in the men's speed event. He was eliminated before the final, ending his first Olympic appearance in the qualification rounds. His family in Xiangxi watched from home, and post-race media coverage highlighted the emotional investment of his hometown community. Teammates described him as consistently composed: "he's always been the same — neither arrogant in victory nor diminished by defeat" (一直都是不卑不亢).

**Future Goals: Breaking 5 Seconds**

Following his 2023 World Championship silver, Long Jinbao stated his intention: "I want to break the 5-second barrier." In 2023, the world's best climbers were approaching and reaching that threshold (world record 4.90s range). For Long, breaking 5 seconds represents both a technical benchmark and a threshold that would bring him into contention for the highest international medals.

**Personal Characteristics**

Long Jinbao's approach to competition has been described as deeply composed. His transition from martial arts instilled in him a mental framework for high-pressure performance — the ability to "屏蔽" (block out) everything external and enter a singular focused state before a climb.
